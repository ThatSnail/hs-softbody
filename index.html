Softbody Physics

Attempt at a very simple softbody engine.

A system is simulated with nodes, connected together by springs. Every timestep, the force of gravity is applied to nodes, and the spring force (by Hooke's law) is applied to nodes connected by lines. A final force, viscous damping, is applied along each spring, proportional to the velocity of the node.

Click one of the buttons below to load a softbody. Drag the mouse in the engine to attract nodes.
